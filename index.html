Interactive algorithmic works

Art that exposes the system making it.

These works are not decorative animations placed beside the gallery; they are living instruments. Each one gives the viewer control over seed, threshold, aperture, color, or contribution, making the image an encounter between rule, perception, and choice. The printable artifact is one state of the system; the interactive page is the organism still breathing.

Accumulation Principle · Instance 1

Residual Warmth

A seeded residual stream system where translucent passes accrue across a persistent paper substrate. The work refuses erasure: each line remains, dims, bends, and contributes.

Philosophy: meaning arrives through sustained attention, not single gesture. Warmth is the visible residue of bounded contribution under constraint.

Accumulation Principle · Instance 2

Ink Blooms

A bloom-based accumulation system where drops become clouds of short radial walks. It shares the same substrate logic as Residual Warmth, but changes the generator from stream to site.

Philosophy: attention does not always travel as a line; sometimes it pools, bleeds, thickens, and makes its own weather around a point.

Aperture Principle

Umwelt

A perceptual-frequency instrument built around Uexküll's self-world: the seed fixes the world, while the aperture determines which slice of reality becomes visible.

Philosophy: the world is not what exists in total; it is what the sensing window admits. Interactivity becomes a perceptual argument.
Open workRead philosophy

As a whole: ARTTRA treats art as a system of encounter rather than a mute object. Static images are artifacts; interactive works are disclosed processes. The viewer does not merely look at a finished thing — they touch the parameters that decide how the thing can become visible.

Lineage: Residual Warmth and Ink Blooms belong to the Accumulation Principle: persistent substrate, bounded additive contribution, structural constraint. Umwelt belongs to an adjacent Aperture Principle: a reality larger than any single rendering, a perceptual operator, and a trace of the excluded.
